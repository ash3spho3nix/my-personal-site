## Overview

Tires exhibit complex non-linear behavior: contact patch evolution, standing waves, mode interactions. This work unified contact mechanics theory with experimental observations.

## Key Contributions

1. **Standing Wave Phenomenon** — In-plane vibration modes in rotating tires can resonate, causing uneven wear and noise.

2. **Contact Pressure Distribution** — Using Hertzian contact theory with finite element refinement to capture:
   - Inflation pressure effects
   - Load history (hysteresis)
   - Temperature-dependent material properties

3. **Resonance Prediction** — Identifying critical speeds where standing waves amplify.

## Application

Results inform:
- Tire design specifications (compounds, geometry)
- Vehicle dynamics models (cornering stiffness, aligning torque)
- NVH diagnostics

## M.Tech Thesis Core

This work formed the foundation of my thesis: rotating rings with application to tire mechanics, combining **rigid body dynamics** (rotation) with **vibration analysis** (deformation).
