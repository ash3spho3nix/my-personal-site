## Overview

Piston rings maintain seal integrity, regulate blow-by, and control oil film thickness. Their dynamics are governed by:
- Radial and axial load distribution
- Gas pressure fluctuation (cycle-dependent)
- Friction and wear
- Elastohydrodynamic lubrication (EHL)

## Modeling Components

1. **Ring-Groove Interaction** — Clearance dynamics, contact forces
2. **Gas Pressure Wave** — From thermodynamic simulation
3. **Oil Film** — Lubrication thickness calculation (minimum film height predicts wear risk)
4. **Stress Analysis** — Peak stresses in ring material

## Outcomes

- Predicted optimal ring face profile for minimal friction
- Identified critical operating points (high speed, high load) where ring flutter occurs
- Validated against engine test data

## Industrial Application

Insights fed into:
- Ring design optimization for next-generation engines
- Wear prediction and maintenance intervals
- Friction reduction targets for fuel efficiency
