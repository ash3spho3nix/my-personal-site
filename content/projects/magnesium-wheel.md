## Challenge

Aluminum wheels are standard for two-wheelers, but magnesium offers:
- 35% weight reduction vs. aluminum (critical for light vehicle dynamics)
- Better damping (vibration absorption)
- Trade-off: corrosion resistance, cost

## Design Approach

1. **Topology Optimization** — Identified optimal material distribution to meet stiffness & strength targets
2. **Fatigue Analysis** — Road-induced loads, worst-case stress concentration
3. **Thermal Analysis** — Brake heating and material limits
4. **Corrosion Mitigation** — Protective coatings specified

## Results

- 40% lighter wheel assembly
- Equivalent fatigue life
- Improved vehicle handling (lower unsprung mass)
- Prototype validation on test rig

## Key Insight

Material substitution requires holistic analysis—weight savings alone don't justify design if reliability suffers.
