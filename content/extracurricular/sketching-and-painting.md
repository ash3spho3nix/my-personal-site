## 🎨 Sketching & Painting

A parallel way of thinking that has nothing to do with engineering — and everything to do with it.

Engineering trains you to decompose problems. Drawing trains you to *see* what's actually there, not what you think is there. These are not the same skill, and the second one is harder.

---

## What It Involves

- Observational drawing — structure, proportion, shadow
- Occasional painting — form and color as information
- Sketchbooks as a thinking tool, not just an art medium

---

## Why It Matters (to Engineering, Oddly)

- Slows down perception — useful when debugging a model that "should work"
- Forces you to look at edge conditions and boundaries, not just the center
- Pattern recognition transfers: a stress concentration in FEA looks a lot like shadow in a drawing

---

## Current Status

Occasional practice. Not consistent enough to brag about, but consistent enough that sketchbooks keep getting filled.
